I am a **Research Fellow** at the **Hyundai–NTU–A\*STAR Corporate Lab**, Nanyang Technological University, supervised by Assoc. Prof. Wai Kin (Adams) Kong. My work bridges **computer vision and robotics** for smart manufacturing, with a focus on open-world detection and segmentation, 6D pose estimation, and grasp pose generation for reliable industrial part picking at HMGICS.

My research centers on robust visual AI systems that can operate under real-world constraints, including degraded media, lightweight deployment, abnormal visual patterns, and previously unseen objects. During my Ph.D. at NTU, advised by Prof. Chau Lap-Pui (IEEE Fellow) and Assoc. Prof. Yap Kim-Hui, I developed deep learning methods for corrupted-media recovery, image super-resolution, and visual anomaly detection.

I have authored 20 peer-reviewed papers in venues including CVPR, NeurIPS, IEEE TMM, ACM MM, and JVCIR, with 300+ citations and an h-index of 8. I also serve as a reviewer for CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, NeurIPS, and ACM MM.

I am open to industry research and applied scientist/engineer opportunities in computer vision, robotic perception, and reliable AI, especially roles that connect research prototypes with real-world intelligent systems.
